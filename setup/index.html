Turn on the Cipher keyboard

About a minute in Settings. Then translate as you type — in Messages, WhatsApp, anywhere.

Takes about one minute

  1. 1

    Open Settings → General

    General
  2. 2

    Tap Keyboard, then Keyboards

    Keyboard
    Keyboards Shows how many you have
  3. 3

    Tap Add New Keyboard… → choose Cipher

    Add New Keyboard…
    Cipher Under Third-Party Keyboards Added
  4. 4

    For translate-as-you-type: turn on Allow Full Access

    Cipher Tap it again in the list
    Allow Full Access

Now use it anywhere

In any app, press and hold the 🌐 globe key on your keyboard, then choose Cipher.

What “Allow Full Access” really does

iOS asks for it because that’s the only way a keyboard can reach the internet — and for Cipher, the only thing it’s used for is translating your text online. Here’s the honest version:

  • The keyboard types fully without Full Access — letters, numbers, emoji, autocorrect. You only need it for online translation.
  • When on, only the text you choose to translate is sent over an encrypted connection — no account, no name, nothing stored.
  • It’s automatically off in password, card-number, and other sensitive fields.
